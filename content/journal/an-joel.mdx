    description_en: |
      For An & Joel, art became the language through which they told their story. Two individuals from different worlds, brought together through contrast, curiosity, and harmony.

      From the very beginning, An believed the wedding invitation would become the first emotional touchpoint for every guest. He entrusted us with a challenge unlike any other: to create a three-dimensional invitation box that embodied the imagined Garden of Eden they had dreamed of together. Every symbolic detail was carefully illustrated by hand, from the Tree of Life and wildflowers to the serpent, forbidden fruit, and two contrasting figures standing side by side. The result felt less like a wedding invitation and more like a collectible work of art.

      While the interior unfolded like a poetic interpretation of the origin of life through a Western artistic lens, the exterior carried subtle Indochine influences inspired by the antique architectural columns of Hoi An. It was only when we finally held the completed invitation in our hands that the emotion truly settled in. After more than four months of relentless dedication, every sketch, revision, and late night had finally bloomed into something tangible and unforgettable.
