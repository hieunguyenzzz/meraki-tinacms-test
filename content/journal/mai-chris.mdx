      Mai and Chris chose the Ho Chi Minh City Museum of Fine Arts as the setting for their wedding, drawn to its architecture that harmoniously blends Eastern and Western artistic influences through Art Deco details. Just moments before the ceremony was due to begin, the bride and our team made a bold decision. Instead of holding the ceremony inside the gallery corridor that had been carefully prepared, we moved it to a small outdoor courtyard. It proved to be exactly the right choice. The rain softened into a gentle drizzle, then stopped completely as the ceremony began.

      Rather than hosting the evening reception outdoors as originally planned, we successfully worked with the museum’s board and resident artists to relocate the dinner into a neighboring exhibition hall. There, guests enjoyed a warm and intimate celebration surrounded by paintings and sculptures. Since the space functions purely as an art gallery, every element of the wedding was thoughtfully brought in from outside. From decor and cuisine to lighting, sound, and even the bar, everything was carefully arranged to transform the room into a welcoming celebration space.
